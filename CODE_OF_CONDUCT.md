# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want EuroMedEval to be useful, rigorous, and welcoming.

Participants are expected to:

- be respectful and constructive
- assume good intent while still giving honest feedback
- document claims about datasets, licensing, and evaluation clearly
- prioritize patient safety and scientific integrity over leaderboard optics

## Unacceptable behavior

- harassment or discrimination
- personal attacks
- intentionally misleading dataset provenance or licensing claims
- submitting fabricated clinical validation or reviewer information

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove or reject contributions that violate this code of conduct or undermine the integrity of the benchmark.
